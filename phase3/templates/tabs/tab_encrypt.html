📝 Step 1 — Read Plaintext Waiting
The original message in UTF-8 bytes.
🎲 Step 2 — Generate Random Nonce (96-bit) Waiting
A cryptographically random 12-byte (96-bit) nonce is generated. It is never reused — a fresh nonce for every single encryption.
🔑 Step 3 — AES-256 Encryption (CTR mode) Waiting
AES encrypts the plaintext using the 256-bit secret key and the nonce in Counter (CTR) mode. No padding is needed — CTR is a stream cipher.
🔏 Step 4 — Compute GHASH Authentication Tag (128-bit) Waiting
The GHASH function computes a 128-bit authentication tag over the ciphertext and the key. This tag is a cryptographic proof of integrity — any modification to the ciphertext will produce a different tag.
📦 Step 5 — Final Output Waiting
Three values are produced. All three are required for decryption.
